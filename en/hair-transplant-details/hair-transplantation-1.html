Hair Mesotherapy


Mesotherapy involves applying cocktails containing vitamins, collagen, biotin, and many other additional substances needed by the hair using micro-needles.

The procedure should be administered by a physician.

Local anesthesia may be used during the procedure to increase patient comfort.

The individual can return to their social life immediately; the procedure lasts for around an hour.

There is no need to shave the hair.

It is not recommended for patients to wash their hair on the same day.

The application frequency varies between 20 to 30 days according to the hair's condition.

A single application may not provide the desired benefit for the patient.

Hair mesotherapy is a treatment that needs to be repeated according to the physician's discretion and the hair's needs.

Hair mesotherapy can be combined with other treatments.

    Questions About Hair Transplantation

    Hair transplantation is the process of transplanting one's own hair follicles to specific areas for individuals experiencing hair loss or thinning. It's a surgical procedure that requires expertise.

    During the procedure, hair follicles are usually extracted from a donor area, often referred to as the scalp's back or sides. These follicles are then carefully placed in areas experiencing hair loss, typically under the skin, to facilitate natural hair growth. The method used is the FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) technique, which involves individually extracting and transplanting hair follicles using microsurgical tools. Hair transplantation may not be suitable for everyone and requires careful evaluation.

    The selection of candidates for hair transplantation depends on each individual's unique health condition and characteristics. However, generally, hair transplantation may not be recommended in the following cases:

    General Health Issues: Individuals with chronic illnesses or serious health problems require a detailed health assessment before considering hair transplantation. Patients with serious health issues like heart diseases, diabetes, high blood pressure, etc., need careful evaluation before such procedures.

    Bleeding Disorders: Individuals with bleeding disorders like hemophilia may not be suitable candidates for hair transplantation due to the risk of abnormal blood clotting during surgical interventions.

    Infectious Diseases: Hair transplantation should not be performed during an active infection. Infections can lead to complications during or after the procedure.

    Underlying Medical Conditions Causing Hair Loss: Hair loss can sometimes be a sign of an underlying serious medical condition. For instance, health issues like hormonal imbalances, thyroid problems, etc., can cause hair loss. Therefore, evaluating such conditions beforehand is crucial.

    Ongoing Hair Loss: If hair loss is a continuously recurring problem, hair transplantation may not be advisable as the transplanted hair can also continue to fall out.

    Inadequate Donor Area: Lack of sufficient donor area for hair transplantation may result in an unsuccessful procedure.

    Each case is unique, and the decision regarding hair transplantation should be made by a specialist doctor considering the patient's individual situation, medical history, and expectations. Therefore, consulting with a professional before any hair transplantation procedure is essential.

    There isn't a mandatory season for hair transplantation, but seasonal factors can bring certain advantages or disadvantages. Here are some points to consider regarding hair transplantation according to seasons:

    Mild Weather Conditions: Mild weather conditions are ideal for hair transplantation. Patients can experience a comfortable recovery period when temperatures are not extremely high or low.

    Spring and Fall: Typically, spring and fall months are ideal for hair transplantation due to mild weather conditions. There's no adverse effect of high temperatures or intense sunlight on the skin.

    Summer Months: Hair transplantation can be done during hot summer months, but one should avoid excessive heat and sun exposure. Prolonged exposure to the sun can negatively affect the recovery process.

    Winter Months: Hair transplantation is possible during cold winter months, but exposure to freezing temperatures and snow-covered areas should be avoided. Additionally, indoor heating systems can cause skin dryness, which should be considered.

    Off-Season Periods: Hair transplantation can be performed in any season. When suitable conditions are ensured, successful procedures can be carried out even during off-season periods.

    An important factor to remember is to undergo a careful recovery process after the hair transplantation procedure. Adhering to the care instructions provided by your doctor is crucial for ensuring success and a speedy recovery.

    In conclusion, there's no mandatory season for hair transplantation. However, the most suitable time should be determined considering the patient's individual preferences, local climate conditions, and recommendations from the surgical team.

    Hair transplanted after the procedure may initially experience shedding, but this is a normal process and usually temporary. This temporary shedding process is called "shock loss" or "telogen effluvium." Here are some details to help understand this process better:

    Shock Loss: Hair follicles are transplanted during the hair transplantation process, which can temporarily send some hair follicles into a resting phase or cause them to shed. It's a stress response for the body, and follicles may remain dormant for a while.

    Temporary Nature: Shock loss is typically observed within the first few weeks after hair transplantation. Hair follicles then start regrowing, and hair begins to grow again.

    Not Permanent: Shock loss doesn't indicate a permanent loss of transplanted hair. On the contrary, hair usually starts growing back within a few months.

    Doctor's Techniques: A doctor performing hair transplantation can employ careful techniques to minimize this process. For instance, the careful extraction and transplantation of hair follicles can reduce the risk of shock loss.

    Recovery Process: The post-transplantation recovery process may take several months for hair to regrow fully and for the results to become apparent. Patience and adherence to the doctor's recommended care instructions are important during this time.

    In conclusion, hair transplanted after the procedure might undergo temporary shedding, but this is usually transient and results in the regrowth of hair. Hair transplantation results generally become more evident within a few months, so patiently following the recovery process is crucial. If you have concerns or if the shedding process lasts longer, it's always a good idea to consult your doctor.

    The number of hair grafts to be implanted is determined based on the individual's current hair condition, the level of hair loss, the quality of the donor area, and the assessment by the hair transplantation specialist. Therefore, consulting a doctor is important to determine the exact number.

    During the hair transplantation process, follicles (hair roots) taken from the donor area are placed in the areas to be transplanted. Each follicle is examined separately during this process, and the areas for transplantation are determined.

    The number of hair grafts implanted during hair transplantation depends on the individual's expectations, the degree of hair loss, and the quantity of hair follicles in the donor area. Typically, thousands of hair grafts can be transplanted. With advanced techniques and experienced surgeons, natural results can be achieved.

    Nevertheless, a detailed assessment by a doctor is necessary to determine how many grafts will be implanted during the hair transplantation procedure. During this evaluation, the patient's level of hair loss, the condition of the donor area, and desired outcomes are taken into account to plan the most suitable approach.

    Hair transplantation is performed using local anesthesia, so most patients generally feel minimal pain or discomfort. Local anesthesia ensures that the donor and transplantation areas are numbed, so patients don't experience pain.

    However, despite the application of local anesthesia, some patients might feel mild discomfort, pressure, or pulling during the procedure. These sensations can vary from person to person. Additionally, changing your position during the procedure or having to stay in the same position for an extended period might cause mild discomfort.

    When hair transplantation is carried out in a hospital setting, sedation is often applied along with local anesthesia to minimize any potential discomfort.

    After the hair transplantation procedure, you might feel slight tightness, itching, or discomfort in the areas where the procedure was performed. However, these types of discomfort are usually short-lived and temporary.

    PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) and Mesotherapy are two different methods commonly used in the treatment of hair loss. Both aim to improve hair health, stop hair loss, and stimulate hair growth. Here are the differences between PRP and Mesotherapy:

    PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma):
    How It Works: PRP involves injecting enriched platelet plasma obtained from the patient's own blood into the scalp. Platelets contain growth factors that promote cell renewal and tissue healing.
    Effects: PRP can stimulate hair follicles, reduce hair loss, and improve hair quality.
    Process: It's usually applied in several sessions with specific intervals between sessions.

    Mesotherapy:
    How It Works: Mesotherapy involves injecting a special mixture into the scalp. This mixture contains vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients that promote hair growth.
    Application: Small injections are made with fine needles directly into the scalp's affected areas.
    Effects: Mesotherapy can nourish the scalp, stimulate hair growth, reduce hair loss, and enhance hair quality.
    Process: It's usually applied in a series of sessions with intervals between sessions.

    Both aim to promote hair growth, reduce loss, and enhance quality, but their techniques and applications differ.

    Both treatments are typically applied in a series of sessions.

    The choice between these treatment methods should be made considering the individual's hair condition, needs, and recommendations from a doctor. Consulting a specialist before application is important.
